"MY JOURNEY"

My Story

Everyone has a story to tell about how they found themselves in the field they’re in today. Often, people say they chose their paths because of passion. I guess my story is a little different. It was never my dream to become a tech person. Although I loved technology and knew how to use a computer as a young Ghanaian kid, my real passion was for health and food. All I dreamed of was becoming a doctor or a chef, because I was (and still am) a good cook. Then came something I didn’t expect — fear. I doubted myself, thinking I wasn’t good enough and afraid that I would fail. I wasn’t ready to face failure. I thought becoming a pilot would be easier, so I decided to take that path instead. From as early as my second year in basic school, I dreamed of flying planes, yet I never stopped learning small things about food, health, and how they work together to heal the body. Before I entered high school, my mother encouraged me to study Biology, one of the subjects I thought was impossible. I told her I wouldn’t do it — not because I didn’t respect her advice, but because I was afraid. Later, I spoke to an alumna about my pilot dream, and she suggested I take ICT as an elective in high school. She said it would help me transition into engineering after school, leading me to become a pilot. So I did. Three years in ICT class often felt like I was in the wrong place — but at least, it wasn’t as demanding as Biology. I completed high school without taking my programming classes seriously, because I believed I was only there to avoid Biology and would later pursue health studies at the university. But life had other plans. When I applied for university, I was denied admission into the health program. The only available option left for me was Computer Science. I felt lost — I didn’t know where to start or how to move forward. Things began to change during my first internship. A fellow intern shared his journey into tech and how he began from zero. His story motivated me to start small, to take my first step — and that’s exactly what I did. That’s how I truly landed in tech. This 90-day challenge is part of that journey — to build myself, grow my skills, and become excellent in what I do. I aim to become a data scientist, but I also want to gain strong foundations in web development, software engineering, and cybersecurity. I believe I can do it — not because I feel completely ready (I’m not), but because I hold on to God’s promise: “I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me.” — Philippians 4:13 I believe He will guide me through this journey, and one day, I’ll become a tech guru who inspires others — especially those who, like me, once felt lost and afraid — to keep pressing on with faith and determination.
